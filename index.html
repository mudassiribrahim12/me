About Me

Download My CV

I am a Ghanaian nurse and researcher whose journey has been shaped by resilience, self-determination, and a deep commitment to improving health outcomes. Inspired by my mother's tireless work as a market trader, I pursued education despite significant obstacles. Although I studied Business (Cost Accounting) in high school, I was determined to become a nurse. I independently taught myself key science subjects, rewrote the required examinations as a private candidate, earned the necessary credentials, enrolled in nursing school, and successfully graduated.

I currently serve at Tamale Teaching Hospital, where I provide high-quality, patient-centered care to individuals with complex medical conditions. Alongside my clinical work, I have built a strong research profileâ€”beginning as a research assistant and growing into an experienced researcher and lead data analyst on multiple population-health projects. My background in business, combined with curiosity and persistence, led me to independently master advanced quantitative methods, including regression modeling, Structural Equation Modeling (SEM), factor analysis, and survival analysis.

Driven by a passion for biostatistics and data-driven solutions, I develop open-source R Shiny web applications that simplify complex analyses for healthcare professionals, students, and researchers. My work bridges clinical practice and data science, reflecting a commitment to both individual patient care and population-level prevention. I aim to advance my training in Epidemiology and Translational Science to better understand disease patterns, strengthen prevention strategies, and contribute to more equitable health systems in Ghana and beyond.
