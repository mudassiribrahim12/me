About Me

Download CV

Mudasir Mohammed Ibrahim is a Ghanaian nurse and researcher whose journey has been shaped by resilience, self-determination, and a deep commitment to improving health outcomes. Inspired by his mother's tireless work as a market trader, he pursued education against significant obstacles. Although he studied Business (Cost Accounting) in high school, he was determined to become a nurse. He independently taught himself key science subjects, rewrote the necessary exams as a private candidate, earned the required credentials, enrolled in nursing school, and successfully graduated.

He now serves at Tamale Teaching Hospital, providing high-quality, patient-centered care to patients with complex medical conditions. Alongside his clinical work, Mudasir has built a strong research career, beginning as a research assistant and growing into an experienced researcher and lead data analyst on multiple population-health projects. His background in business, coupled with his curiosity and determination, led him to independently master advanced quantitative methods, including regression modeling, Structural Equation Modeling (SEM), factor analysis, and survival analysis.

Motivated by a passion for biostatistics and data-driven solutions, he has developed several open-source R Shiny web applications to simplify complex analyses for healthcare professionals, students, and researchers. His work bridges clinical practice and data science, reflecting a commitment to both individual patient care and population-level prevention. Mudasir aims to advance his expertise in Epidemiology and Translational Science to better understand disease patterns, strengthen prevention strategies, and contribute to equitable health systems in Ghana and beyond.
